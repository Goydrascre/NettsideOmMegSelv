Cork City

Cork is Ireland’s second largest city and is known for its warm, welcoming atmosphere and vibrant cultural scene. Located on the Banks of River Lee, Corks prides itself on its famous markets like the english market, and its historical landmarks. Here are some of the places that we recommend you visit.

Blarney Castle

Blarney Castle Blarney Castle has stood imposingly for over 6 centuries, attracting tourists from all over, not just because of its beautiful medieval architecture, but also because of its history. There is no guide so you are free to explore wherever you want, whether that be the infamous poison garden, or taking part in the couragus “gift of gab”, a ritual where you hang upside down from the top of the castle, and kiss the wall. This feat supposedly gives you the gift of eloquence, which in layman's terms means the ability to speak persuasively.

Rebel Distillery

Rebel Distillery, located in the heart of Cork, is the first distillery Ireland has seen in 50 years! Here, you are taken on a tour through the craft distillery, founded in 2020, and taste the delicious wine crafted with a mix of modern and old brewing techniques that Ireland is known for.

St. Fin Barre Cathedral

St. Fin Barre Cathedral is a beautiful gothic revival cathedral dedicated to the patron saint of the city, fin barre. Completed in 1879, the cathedral’s interior features intricate stone carvings on the walls, and mesmerizing stained glass windows, with the main attraction in the room being the ginormous organ with over 4000 pipes! The exterior is not to shun either as it is covered with spires, gargoyles and a golden angel displayed on top of the center tower.
