Limerick City

Limerick is a historic city, known for its museums, castle and hiking trails. It offers a rich cultural experience with its blend of historic places and modern charm. We have listed three of the places that you have to experience

King John's Castle

King John's Castle, otherwise known as Limerick Castle is one of the best preserved castles in Ireland. Built in the early 13th century, the castle offers a glimpse into medieval life within its giant stone walls. Today, the castle is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Limerick and for good reason. It features both interactive exhibits with fun animations, and a cafe.

The flying boat museum

The flying boat museum located in Foynes celebrates Limerick's pivotal role in the development of modern airplanes. During the 1930s and 1940s, Foynes was a hub for transatlantic flights, serving as a key stop for flying boats crossing between Europe and North America. The museum showcases this period with interactive exhibits like trying your hand at flying the Boeing 314 yourself in their highly realistic flight simulator and if that's not real enough for you then you can step into the only Boeing 314 full scale replica in the world!

The coolwater garden

The coolwater garden, located in Limerick, allows you a nice break from the action of the bustling city. The garden features a serene pond, covered in water lilies and outputting a calming aura together with the sound of the waterfall making riplets in the center pond.
