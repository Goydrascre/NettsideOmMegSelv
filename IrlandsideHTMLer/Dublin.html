Dublin City

Dublin is the capital of Ireland and is most known for its nightlife, museums and parks. Dublin is also the biggest city in Ireland so we have picked out some spots for you to visit so you don't feel lost.

Kilmainham gaol

The Kilmainham gaol had for over a hundred years held prisoners that range from pesky thieves to the people who have taken part in some of the biggest events in Irish history. A visit to Kilmainham Gaol will take you on a tour through these events, providing insight into the harsh conditions faced by the average criminal as well as the heroes who fought for Ireland's independence during the 1798 Rebellion, the 1916 Easter Rising, the Anglo-Irish War from 1919 to 1921, and the tragic Irish Civil War of 1922-1923. After your trip here, we're certain that you'll see Ireland in a new light, and earn a newfound respect for the country, and its legacy.

St.Stephens Green

St. Stephens Green is a beautiful public park located in the centre of Dublin City. The square park lies adjacent to one of Dublin's biggest shopping streets, which makes it the perfect place to eat your snacks. The park's development spaned for over 200 years, Starting construction as far back as 1664 and finally opening to the public in July of 1880. The history of the park is a rich one, and you can feel it while walking through the luscious flora, or relaxing on a bench listening to the birds chirp happily. A trip to St. Stephens Green is a must for anyone needing to take a break from the busy travelling and wants to take a minute to be with nature.
