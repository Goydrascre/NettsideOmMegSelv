Dublin City

Dublin is the capital of Ireland and is most known for its nightlife, museums, and parks. Dublin is also the biggest city in Ireland, so we have picked out some spots for you to visit so you don't feel lost.

Kilmainham Gaol

Kilmainham Gaol held prisoners for over a hundred years, from petty thieves to those involved in significant events in Irish history. A visit to Kilmainham Gaol will take you on a tour through these events, providing insight into the harsh conditions faced by the average criminal as well as the heroes who fought for Ireland's independence during the 1798 Rebellion, the 1916 Easter Rising, the Anglo-Irish War from 1919 to 1921, and the tragic Irish Civil War of 1922-1923. After your trip here, we're certain that you'll see Ireland in a new light and gain newfound respect for the country and its legacy.

St. Stephen's Green

St. Stephen's Green is a beautiful public park located in the centre of Dublin City. The square park lies adjacent to one of Dublin's biggest shopping streets, making it the perfect place to enjoy some snacks. The park's development spanned over 200 years, starting construction as far back as 1664 and finally opening to the public in July of 1880. The history of the park is rich, and you can feel it while walking through the lush flora or relaxing on a bench listening to the birds chirping happily. A trip to St. Stephen's Green is a must for anyone needing a break from busy travel and wanting to reconnect with nature.

Whiskey Tasting Museum

When you think of Irish culture, chances are whiskey is one of the first things that come to mind—and for good reason. At Dublin’s Whiskey Tasting Museum, you'll embark on a journey through renowned distilleries and the unique art of whiskey making. Of course, you'll also have the chance to taste some of the most famous Irish whiskeys. The tour provides insight into why whiskey is so important to the Irish and how it rose to global fame.
