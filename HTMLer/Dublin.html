Dublin City

Dublin is the capital of Ireland and is most known for its nightlife, museums and parks. Dublin is also the biggest city in Ireland so we have picked out some spots for you to visit so you don't feel lost.

Killmainham gaol

The Killmainham gaol had for over a hundred years held prisoners that range from pesky thieves, to the people that have taken part in some of the biggest events through Irish history. A visit to The killmainham gaol will take you on a tour through these events, and you’ll get knowledge on the harsh conditions that the average criminal faced here, aswell as the heroes who fought for the Independance of Ireland in the 1798 rebelion, the 1916 easter rising, the Angelo-Irish war in 1919-21, and of course the tragety that is the Irish civil war of 1922-23. After your trip here, we're certain that you'll see Ireland in a new light, and earn a newfound respect for the country, and its legacy.

St.Stephens Green

St. Stephens Green is a beautiful public park located in the center of Dublin city. The square park lays adjacent to one of Dublins biggest shopping streets, which makes it the perfect place to eat your snacks at. The park has been in development for over 200 years! starting construction as far back as in 1664 and finally opening to the public in july of 1880. The history of the park is a rich one, and you can feel it while walking through the lushious flora, or relaxing on a bench listening to the birds chirp happily. A trip to St. Stephens Green is a must for anyone needing to take a break from the busy traveling, and wants to take a minute to be with nature.

Whiskey tasting museum

When you think of Irish culture, chances are that wiskey is one of the first thing you think of, and for good reason. In Dublin’s Whiskey tasting museum you’ll go on a journey through the famous distilleries and the unique art of whiskey making, and of course you’ll be able to get a taste of all the most well known wine. The tour will also grant you knowledge on why Wine is so important for The Irish, and its rise to global fame.
